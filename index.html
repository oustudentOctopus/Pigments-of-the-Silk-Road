The Pigments of the Silk Road: Interactive Sogdian Wall Painting

This is an interactive version of the South wall mural at the palace in Afrasiab, Samarkand (650 CE)

Click on a part of the image to view information about the pigments used.

Intro

This mural is a part of the large murals covering all four walls of the reception hall of the palace in Samarkand. This section is a part of the South wall, which shows a funerary precession. The painting is an example of high-end murals, made with quality pigments and techniques of painters along the Silk Road. Based on the quality of its construction and composition, and on the level of detail and fine pigments used, it is safe to assume this mural was costly to create, and that the artists hired were fine craftsmen.

The painting was first outlined in red, since it would show through less than black on the finished work. The pigments were then applied dry, onto a tree gum-based binder. This technique provided a long working time and allowed the artists to create clean lines and very fine details which would have been difficult with a brush.
